About


I am a writer of contemporary and period drama features and pilots that have placed at the quarterfinal level or above in the Austin Film Festival, PAGE International, BlueCat and Creative World Awards screenwriting competitions.

I also read for a major screenwriting competition.

My dark-comedy drama feature BETWEEN LINES placed as a Quarterfinalist in the BlueCat and Creative World Awards screenwriting competitions.

I was a 2018 Semifinalist in the Austin Film Festival's scriptwriting competition for my period drama feature LEAH ROSE. In the words of one festival judge, LEAH ROSE "is a masterful work that deserves to be recognized and produced." 

Most recently, my drama pilot FISHTOWN placed as a Finalist in the 2019 Creative World Awards, and as a Quarterfinalist in the 2019 PAGE Screenwriting Awards.

Features

LEAH ROSE

Drama | Feature | 108 pages

In Depression-era New York, a socialite pursues a professional piano career as she learns to live poor while her husband does time for bootlegging.

This script was inspired by my interest in history, particularly the years between the First and Second World Wars, and favorite films CABARET, CHARIOTS OF FIRE, REDS and THE REMAINS OF THE DAY that so compellingly brought that era to life.

Driven by a strong female lead, and set during the precarious opening years of the Great Depression with the ominous threat of nationalism on the horizon, the economic, social and political themes at the heart of this story have just as much to say about the world of today as they do about the past.

A semifinalist in the Austin Film Festival's prestigious scriptwriting competition, LEAH ROSE was described by one of its judges as "a masterful work that deserves to be recognized and produced." 

BETWEEN LINES

Drama | Feature | 90 pages

It's a race against time for a frustrated writer attempting to mend his wild ways before an upcoming high school reunion after he learns of a former classmate's recent splash in the literary world; but is she a rival he seeks to impress or the one who got away?

FISHTOWN

Drama | Pilot | 68 pages

A talented young chef and his business-savvy wife try to turn a local dive into the hot new spot in a gentrifying neighborhood, but the fledgling restaurateurs may have bitten off more than they can chew.

HOUSE MOM

Drama | Pilot | 62 pages

When an executive coach struggling to attract more female clients is compelled to take over management of the dressing room at the strip club where she moonlights as a bartender, she discovers a new outlet for her skills by helping the club's dancers survive the perils of the pole.
